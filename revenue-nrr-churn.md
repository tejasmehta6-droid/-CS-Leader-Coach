# Revenue, NRR & Churn Coaching Reference

## The Commercial Reality of CS

CS leaders who win at the board level think like revenue leaders, not service leaders.
The framing shift: **CS is the revenue protection and growth engine, not the support layer.**

NRR is the single metric that defines CS's commercial value:
- NRR < 90% → CS is a cost center losing ground
- NRR 90–100% → CS is maintaining, not growing
- NRR 100–110% → CS is a healthy revenue contributor
- NRR 110%+ → CS is a growth engine; the company can grow even with zero new logos

## Churn Situations

### Diagnosing Churn (Always Do This First)

Before any churn save motion, diagnose the real reason. Price is almost never the real reason.

**The 5 real causes of churn:**
1. **Value gap** — customer never achieved the outcome they bought for (TTV failure)
2. **Champion loss** — your internal advocate left or lost influence
3. **Product-market drift** — their needs evolved, your product didn't keep up
4. **Competitive displacement** — a competitor offered something meaningfully better
5. **Economic pressure** — budget cuts (this is the one case where price IS the reason)

**Diagnostic questions to ask the customer:**
> "Help me understand — if budget wasn't a factor at all, would you continue with us?"
> "Walk me through what success would have looked like. Did we get there?"
> "Who else at your org was involved in this decision?"

### Churn Save Playbook

**Stage 1: Immediate (Days 1–3)**
- Get the VP CS or a senior leader on a call. Not the CSM alone.
- Goal: understand, not pitch. Listen more than you talk.
- Script: *"We're not here to convince you of anything today. We want to understand what we got wrong and what it would take — if anything — to earn another shot."*

**Stage 2: Internal Response (Days 3–7)**
- Map the real stakeholders — who influenced this decision?
- Identify if there's a commercial lever (pricing, contract restructure) — only use if value gap is closed first
- Build a "recovery plan" document: specific commitments, timelines, owners

**Stage 3: Executive Engagement (Week 2)**
- If ARR is significant, VP CS or CCO calls their exec sponsor directly
- Lead with accountability: *"We didn't deliver what we promised. Here's specifically what we're changing."*
- Present the recovery plan — don't just talk about it

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ Offer a discount before understanding the real problem
- ❌ Send the CSM alone to a churn conversation they didn't see coming
- ❌ Promise roadmap features you don't control
- ❌ Let it go more than 48 hours without a response plan

### At-Risk Account Playbook

**Early warning signals (leading indicators):**
- Login frequency drops >30% over 4 weeks
- Champion goes silent (stops responding, misses calls)
- Support tickets spike (frustration signals)
- QBR no-show or repeated reschedules
- New exec stakeholder appears without introduction
- Competitor mentioned in conversation

**Response by risk level:**

| Risk Level | Signal | Response |
|-----------|--------|----------|
| Yellow | 1–2 signals | CSM proactive outreach within 48hrs |
| Orange | 3+ signals or champion loss | VP CS involvement, executive call scheduled |
| Red | Churn notice or explicit dissatisfaction | War room: CS + AE + exec, daily check-in |

## NRR Improvement Plays

### Expansion Motion
The biggest NRR lever most CS teams underuse.

**When to surface expansion:**
- Customer hits a usage milestone (seats, API calls, data volume)
- New use case emerges in QBR/EBR
- Champion gets promoted (new authority = new budget)
- Product launches something their adjacent team needs
- Health score turns green after a rocky start (best time to expand)

**How to hand off to Sales (CS-Qualified Lead):**
Script for CSM to Sales AE:
> *"I'm flagging [Customer] as expansion-ready. [Specific trigger]. The champion [Name] mentioned [specific need] last week. They're receptive — I'd recommend a call this month before their Q[X] budget cycle closes."*

**What NOT to do:**
- ❌ Surface expansion during an active at-risk situation
- ❌ Let Sales lead expansion without CS involvement (breaks trust)
- ❌ Wait for the renewal to have the expansion conversation

### Renewal Playbook

**90 days out:**
- Renewal review internally: health score, expansion potential, risk flags
- CSM + AE alignment call: who owns what in the renewal conversation?
- Begin surfacing value narrative: pull ROI data, usage stats, outcomes achieved

**60 days out:**
- EBR or value review with customer executive — not a renewal pitch, a value confirmation
- Script: *"We're approaching your renewal and I wanted to make sure we've captured the full value picture from this year before we talk contracts."*

**30 days out:**
- Commercial conversation — pricing, terms, multi-year options
- If at-risk: escalate immediately, don't wait

**The renewal conversation frame:**
Don't lead with the contract. Lead with: *"Here's what you've achieved. Here's what's next. Here's how we continue."*

## Metrics & Reporting

### NRR Reporting to Leadership

**The formula:**
NRR = (Starting ARR + Expansion − Contraction − Churn) / Starting ARR × 100

**How to present NRR in a board narrative:**
1. NRR trend (not just snapshot) — 3 or 4 quarter view
2. Churn decomposed: logo vs. revenue, avoidable vs. unavoidable
3. Expansion contribution: how much of NRR came from expansion vs. pure retention
4. Cohort view: which customer segments perform best and why

**The insight executives want:**
Not just "NRR is 104%" — but "here's what's driving it, here's the risk concentration, and here's what we're doing about it."

### Health Score Design

**Common mistake:** Using health score as a lagging indicator (it shows you what already happened).

**Better design:** Build health scores with 70%+ weight on leading indicators:
- Product engagement trends (not just current state)
- Stakeholder relationship breadth
- Time since last meaningful business conversation
- Support sentiment trend

**AI-enhanced health scoring:**
Modern CS platforms (Gainsight, ChurnZero, Totango) are integrating AI to predict churn 90–120 days out. If you're not using predictive churn signals alongside your static health scores, you're flying half-blind.
